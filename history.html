Historical Timeline

  1. 1972 – SCCS: Early change-tracking for single files.
  2. 1982 – RCS: Improved revision control with delta storage.
  3. 1986 – CVS: Centralized system enabling team collaboration.
  4. 2000 – Subversion: Enhanced centralized control with atomic commits.
  5. 2005 – Git & Mercurial: Introduced distributed version control.
  6. 2008 – GitHub: Added hosting and social collaboration features.

Why Distributed VCS Changed the Game

  • Every developer has a complete local repository.
  • Branching and merging are fast, flexible, and encouraged.
  • Supports multiple remotes — no single point of failure.
  • Offline work and faster local operations.
  • Decentralized collaboration revolutionized open-source workflows.
